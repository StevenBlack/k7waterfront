THE LACK OF SHORELINE FISHING SPOTS is the subject of an interesting front-page story in today's Whig. It highlights a big problem with the waterfront in the City of Kingston: accessibility.

We've got:

  • town docks we can't walk,
  • birds-only breakwalls,
  • several ramps with little or no parking for trailers,
  • apparently numbskulls are in charge at Fort Henry and,
  • according to the article, angler-hostile desk-flying bureaucrats at all levels.
